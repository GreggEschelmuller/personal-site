About

A movement scientist focused on sensory feedback and learning.

I work across motor control, motor learning, proprioception, and sensorimotor physiology.

Professional profile

I completed a PhD in Kinesiology with a focus on motor control, motor learning, proprioception, and sensorimotor physiology. My research examines how the nervous system uses sensory feedback to estimate body position, adapt movement, and maintain control when feedback changes.

My work combines human experiments, neuromechanical measurement, computational modeling, and practical analysis workflows. I care about careful experiments, clear explanation, and methods that other people can understand and use.

Working focus

  • Research on proprioception, kinesthesia, tendon vibration, and sensorimotor adaptation.
  • Teaching in physiology, motor behavior, neuroanatomy, and neuromechanical kinesiology.
  • Applied analysis using experimental systems, signal processing, modeling, and reproducible research workflows.
