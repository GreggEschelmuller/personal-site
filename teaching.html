Teaching

Teaching movement science.

I teach physiology, motor behavior, neuroanatomy, and neuromechanics, with an emphasis on connecting mechanisms to movement people can recognize.

Teaching approach

Start with the movement, then build the mechanism.

Movement science can become abstract quickly. My teaching uses concrete examples, careful definitions, diagrams, and practice with real physiological and behavioral data.

Physiology

Systems physiology and human function, with attention to how structure supports behavior.

Motor behavior

Motor control and motor learning as practical frameworks for skill, adaptation, and performance.

Neuroanatomy

Nervous system organization for understanding movement, sensation, and coordination.

Mentorship

Guidance in experimental design, data analysis, scientific writing, and research workflows.
